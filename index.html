Why this matters for dealerships

The dealership stack is no longer just about websites, DMS, IMS, CRM, voice agents and marketing automation. It increasingly depends on connected service events, remote diagnostics, consent flows and integrations between OEM systems and third-party platforms.

1. Data access becomes strategic

Dealers and their technology partners need to think beyond traditional lead and transaction data. The ability to consume and operationalise vehicle-generated data can affect aftersales, maintenance offers, fleet services, subscriptions and customer retention.

2. Integration architecture gets more important

Middleware, APIs, consent records, identity layers and governance workflows become critical. The question is not only who sees the data, but how data moves across DMS, CRM, analytics, service scheduling and customer applications.

3. Compliance and product design converge

Product teams serving dealerships need to design for transparency, secure access and role-based data sharing. Legal interpretation, user permissions and technical implementation increasingly need to be built together from day one.
